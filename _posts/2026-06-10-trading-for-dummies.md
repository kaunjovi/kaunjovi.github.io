## QSR stands for Quick Service Restaurant

QSR. Fast food, organized. 

In plain English, it is the organized fast-food sector—counter-service restaurants where you order, pay, and get your food within minutes. Think Domino’s, KFC, McDonald's, Pizza Hut, Burger King, and also homegrown chains like Haldiram's (to an extent).

The Core Business Model (The Good & The Ugly)
Standardization is King: These businesses succeed not by culinary excellence, but by consistency. 
A Domino's pizza tastes the same in Mumbai, Delhi, or Indore. This allows massive scale.

High Operating Leverage: Rent (fixed), staff salaries (semi-fixed), and advertising are their biggest costs. 
Once a store breaks even, every extra rupee of revenue flows disproportionately to the bottom line. 
However, if revenue slows, those fixed costs crush the P&L—exactly what is happening to Devyani right now.

Working Capital Magic: Most QSRs collect cash upfront from customers (or delivery apps) but pay their suppliers on 30-60 day terms. This generates negative working capital—free cash to fund expansion.

Urban Demand Slowdown: The core customer base is the urban, aspirational middle class. Inflation in cereals, vegetables, and fuel has pinched their disposable income. The first thing they cut is eating out or ordering in.

Delivery Aggregator Dominance: Zomato and Swiggy now take 20-25% commissions. QSRs are forced to offer deep discounts to stay visible on these platforms, destroying their per-order profitability.

Store Saturation / Cannibalization: Chains went on a massive store-opening spree post-COVID (2022-24). Now, new stores are merely stealing customers from existing ones. Same-Store Sales Growth (SSSG)—the holy grail metric for QSRs—has turned negative or flat for almost every major player over the last 3-4 quarters.

Commodity Inflation: Prices of wheat, edible oils, and poultry (chicken) remain volatile. Unlike FMCG (like your Marico or Pidilite), QSRs cannot easily pass on price hikes without losing footfall.

# GBR-BAHRE Portfolio Management Framework

## 1. The GBR Sector Conviction Scale

| Color | Tag | Definition | Sector Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GREEN** | **G** | Highest Conviction. Structural tailwinds, strong earnings visibility, fair valuation. | Earnings growth >15% CAGR over 2-3 years; Structural macro driver (capex, policy, demographics); Valuation at or below historical average P/E; Positive institutional consensus; Low macro risk relative to peers. |
| **BLUE** | **B** | Neutral Conviction. Steady growth, fairly valued, no major catalyst. | Earnings growth 8-12%; Valuation in line with historical average; No strong re-rating catalyst; No major downside risk; Offers stability and steady compounding. |
| **RED** | **R** | Lowest Conviction. Structural headwinds, overvalued, or broken thesis. | Earnings growth <8% or declining; Valuation >20% above historical average; Negative institutional consensus; High exposure to macro risks (commodity, geopolitical, AI disruption); Structural decline risk. |

## 3. The Action Scale (BAHRE - Derived from Weight Gaps)

**Conviction (GBR) is Strategic. Action (BAHRE) is Tactical.**

Action is determined purely by comparing **Current Weight** vs. **Target Weight** for each sector/stock.

| Color Rating | Current Weight vs. Target | Action | Intensity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **GREEN** | Far below target (Gap >5%) | **BUY** | Aggressively deploy capital |
| **GREEN** | Slightly below (Gap 2-5%) | **ACCUMULATE** | Add on dips, in tranches |
| **GREEN / BLUE / RED** | At target (Gap within +/-2%) | **HOLD** | Do nothing |
| **GREEN** | Above target (Gap >2%) | **REDUCE** | Trim excess, rebalance |
| **BLUE** | Far below/at/above target | **ACCUMULATE / HOLD / REDUCE** | Cautiously adjust |
| **RED** | Any holding exists | **REDUCE / SELL** | Exit completely or trim aggressively |

**Note:** If a sector is RED, the target weight is 0%. Any holding must be reduced to zero.

## 6. Golden Rules

1. **Conviction ≠ Action.** GREEN does not mean "always buy". GREEN means "high conviction". Action is determined by the gap between current allocation and target allocation.
2. **Rebalance to Target.** If a GREEN sector exceeds its target band by >5%, trim the excess and redeploy into an underweight GREEN sector.
3. **Minimize Churn.** Wealth creation is the priority. Frequent churn generates short-term capital gains tax and breaks the compounding curve.
4. **All new capital flows to GREEN first.** BLUE sectors are maintained, not aggressively added to. RED sectors are exited.
5. **Review Colors Quarterly.** Update sector ratings post-results season (Jul, Oct, Jan, Apr). Review allocations monthly.
